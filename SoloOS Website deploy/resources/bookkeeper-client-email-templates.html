10 client email templates every bookkeeper rewrites

Bookkeepers do not need to invent fresh language every time a receipt is missing, a transaction is unclear, or a month-end summary needs to go out. Start with clean reusable templates, then adjust the details.

Use these as starting language, not final advice. Keep client data private, verify every fact, and avoid giving tax, legal, payroll, or compliance advice unless you are qualified to do so.

1. Missing receipt email

Use when the books are mostly ready, but a few receipts or documents are blocking cleanup.

Subject: Missing receipts for [month] Hi [Client Name], I am working through [month] and need a few missing receipts before I can finish the review. Could you please send receipts or notes for: - [Transaction 1] - [Transaction 2] - [Transaction 3] If you do not have a receipt, a quick note explaining what the purchase was for is still helpful. Thank you, [Your Name]

2. Uncategorized transaction email

Use when bank feed descriptions are too vague to classify safely.

Subject: Quick questions on uncategorized transactions Hi [Client Name], I have a few transactions from [month] that need clarification before I categorize them. Could you tell me what these were for? - [Date] - [Vendor] - [Amount] - [Date] - [Vendor] - [Amount] - [Date] - [Vendor] - [Amount] A short description is fine. I mainly need to know the business purpose. Thank you, [Your Name]

3. Personal expense boundary email

Use when business and personal spending are mixed together.

Subject: Business and personal expenses Hi [Client Name], I noticed a few transactions that look personal rather than business-related. I have set them aside for review so the business records stay clean. For future months, the cleanest process is to keep business purchases on the business account/card whenever possible. It makes reports more accurate and reduces cleanup time. For now, could you confirm whether these were business or personal? - [Transaction list] Thank you, [Your Name]

4. Monthly bookkeeping summary email

Use after completing a monthly review.

Subject: [Month] bookkeeping summary Hi [Client Name], [Month] bookkeeping is complete. Here is the quick summary: - Income reviewed: [status] - Expenses reviewed: [status] - Bank/credit card reconciliation: [status] - Open questions: [number] - Items needing your attention: [brief list] The main thing to watch this month is [plain-English note]. Thank you, [Your Name]

5. Cleanup project status update

Use when a cleanup is moving but not finished yet.

Subject: Cleanup progress update Hi [Client Name], I wanted to send a quick update on the cleanup. Completed so far: - [Task completed] - [Task completed] - [Task completed] Still in progress: - [Open item] - [Open item] What I need from you next: - [Client action item] Once I have that, I can move to the next stage of the cleanup. Thank you, [Your Name]

6. Scope boundary email

Use when the client asks for work outside the original agreement.

Subject: About the additional request Hi [Client Name], I can help with that, but it falls outside the scope of our current bookkeeping work. The current scope covers [current scope]. The new request would require [additional work]. If you would like, I can send a separate estimate or add-on option for that work before I begin. Thank you, [Your Name]

7. Late payment reminder

Use for a polite first reminder.

Subject: Reminder: invoice [invoice number] Hi [Client Name], This is a quick reminder that invoice [invoice number] for [amount] is currently outstanding. You can pay using the link here: [payment link] Please let me know if you need the invoice resent or if there is anything I should update. Thank you, [Your Name]

8. Cash-flow warning email

Use carefully when the numbers show a pattern the owner needs to see.

Subject: Cash flow note from this month Hi [Client Name], While reviewing [month], I noticed a pattern worth watching: [plain-English observation]. This does not automatically mean something is wrong, but it is worth looking at before it becomes harder to correct. The next useful step would be to review: - [Item 1] - [Item 2] - [Item 3] Let me know if you would like me to prepare a clearer breakdown for our next review. Thank you, [Your Name]

9. Client action list email

Use when the client needs one simple list instead of scattered questions.

Subject: Bookkeeping action list Hi [Client Name], To keep the books moving, here are the items I need from you: 1. [Action item] 2. [Action item] 3. [Action item] If possible, please send these by [date] so I can finish [month/cleanup/project]. Thank you, [Your Name]

10. AI-safe rewrite prompt

Use this when you want AI to improve tone without exposing client data.

Prompt: You are helping a bookkeeper rewrite a client email. Rewrite the email below so it is clear, professional, and not annoyed. Use placeholders only. Do not invent facts. Do not give tax, legal, payroll, accounting, or compliance advice. Email draft: [Paste a sanitized draft with no client names, company names, account numbers, tax IDs, bank data, payroll data, or real transaction lists.]
The real time saver is not one template. It is having a repeatable communication system: missing-items emails, cleanup notes, monthly summaries, report explanations, and safe AI prompts that do not touch private client data.
